What Are Rheology Modifiers for Personal Care?

Feb. 08, 2025

Share:

1. Understanding Rheology Modifiers and Their Importance


Rheology modifiers are essential ingredients used in personal care products to control and adjust the viscosity, texture, and flow behavior of formulations. These substances enable manufacturers to create products with the desired consistency, stability, and sensory attributes, improving both the application process and consumer experience.


In personal care, rheology modifiers are vital for achieving the following:


  • Enhanced texture: Making lotions smooth, gels firm, and shampoos flowable.

  • Suspension capabilities: Preventing active ingredients, such as exfoliants or pigments, from settling.

  • Improved stability: Ensuring products maintain their properties during storage and use.

  • Optimized sensory feel: Tailoring how a product feels during application, whether silky, rich, or lightweight.

 

2. Common Types of Rheology Modifiers and Their Applications

 

Rheology modifiers can be broadly categorized into two types based on their origin: natural and synthetic. Each type has unique characteristics and benefits, making them suitable for different applications.

 

Synthetic Rheology Modifiers

  • Carbomers (e.g., Carbomer 940, 980, 996, 676): Highly efficient thickeners used in gels, creams, and lotions. They create clear and stable gels, making them ideal for products like hand sanitizers, facial gels, and anti-aging serums.

  • Acrylates/C10-30 Alkyl Acrylate Crosspolymer: A versatile polymer that provides excellent thickening, stabilization, and suspension properties, especially in formulations with challenging active ingredients.

  • Acrylates Copolymer: Known for its ability to deliver smooth textures and improve product aesthetics, it is often used in shampoos and conditioners.

 

Natural Rheology Modifiers

  • Hydroxyethyl Cellulose (HEC): A water-soluble polymer derived from cellulose, commonly used in shampoos, body washes, and facial cleansers for its thickening and film-forming properties.

  • Hydroxypropyl Methyl Cellulose (HPMC): A multifunctional ingredient used in creams, lotions, and sunscreens. It provides both thickening and emulsifying capabilities, making it ideal for stabilizing emulsions.

  • PEG-120 Methyl Glucose Dioleate: A plant-derived thickener that offers mildness and a silky feel, often included in sulfate-free formulations like baby shampoos and sensitive-skin cleansers.


At GOLIATH, we provide all these rheology modifiers to cater to diverse personal care needs.


 


3. Comparing Natural and Synthetic Rheology Modifiers


Natural Rheology Modifiers

Advantages:

  • Derived from renewable sources, making them more eco-friendly.

  • Perceived as safer and gentler by consumers, aligning with trends like "clean beauty."

Disadvantages:

  • It may have limited thickening efficiency compared to synthetic options.

  • It can be less stable in extreme pH or temperature conditions.


Synthetic Rheology Modifiers

Advantages:

  • Highly effective in small quantities, offering superior thickening and stabilization.

  • Greater compatibility with a wide range of actives and formulation conditions.

Disadvantages:

  • Typically derived from petroleum-based resources.

  • It may not align with consumer demand for natural or sustainable ingredients.


At GOLIATH, we recognize the importance of offering both natural and synthetic rheology modifiers. This allows our customers to choose products that align with their formulation goals and market demands.

 

 


4. Choosing the Right Rheology Modifier for Product Development


Selecting the right rheology modifier is a critical step in personal care product development. Key factors to consider include:


  • Product type: A lightweight serum requires a thickener different from that of rich body butter.

  • Formulation stability: Consider the pH, ionic strength, and interactions with other ingredients.

  • Desired sensory experience: For example, Carbomer 940 creates a light, non-greasy feel, while PEG-120 Methyl Glucose Dioleate adds a silky, luxurious texture.

  • Regulatory requirements: Ensure compliance with local and international regulations, especially for natural and "clean beauty" products.

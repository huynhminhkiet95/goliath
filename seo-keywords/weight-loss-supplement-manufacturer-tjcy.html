At GOLIATH, we supply a diverse portfolio of scientifically supported active ingredients for weight management. These ingredients are commonly used in prescription anti-obesity drugs, dietary supplements, and functional formulations. Based on their mechanisms of action, our ingredients are typically categorized into three major classes:

 

1. Centrally Acting Appetite Suppressants

 

These ingredients target the central nervous system (CNS) to regulate hunger and satiety. By influencing neurotransmitters like serotonin, dopamine, or norepinephrine, they help reduce food intake by promoting a feeling of fullness or decreasing the psychological desire to eat.

They are commonly used in medical weight loss therapies or appetite control supplements.

Setmelanotide

Targets rare genetic obesity via MC4R pathway

 

2. Peripherally Acting Agents: Reduce Absorption or Boost Metabolism

  

These ingredients do not act directly on the brain, but instead control weight by inhibiting nutrient absorption or increasing the body's metabolic rate. Common effects include inhibiting digestive enzymes (such as lipase), improving insulin sensitivity, stimulating thermogenesis, etc., and are suitable for use in fat absorption inhibitors, carbohydrate inhibitors, or metabolic regulation products.

 

  • Liraglutide

  • Semaglutide

  • Tirzepatide

  • Orlistat

  • Colesevelam

  • Pramlintide

  • Mirabegron

 

GOLIATH supplies high-purity raw materials in multiple forms, including freeze-dried powder and sterile-grade APIs, suitable for use in injectable, oral, and transdermal formulations. Custom specifications are available upon request.

 

3. Thermogenic & Energy Expenditure Enhancers

 

These active substances help the body burn more energy at rest or during exercise by activating brown adipose tissue (BAT), enhancing mitochondrial activity, or increasing the basal metabolic rate, and are often used in sports nutrition and clinical weight loss programs.

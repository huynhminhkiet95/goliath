1. Topical Excipients

Topical excipients are essential for external preparations such as ointments, creams, and gels. These excipients enhance the absorption and stability of the active pharmaceutical ingredients (APIs).

 

Castor Oil

Usage: Used in ointments and creams to enhance drug penetration.

Function: Increases drug absorption, improving therapeutic effects.

 

Vaseline

Usage: Used in skin protection ointments, providing moisture and barrier functions.

Function: Helps stabilize drugs and prevent skin dryness.

 

Paraffin

Usage: Common in ointments and creams, improving drug stability.

Function: Enhances the consistency and applicability of the product.

 

2. Antibacterial Preservatives & Antiseptics

These excipients are crucial for preventing microbial contamination of pharmaceutical products, ensuring the safety and longevity of the drugs.

 

Phenoxyethanol

Usage: Used as a preservative in skin ointments, lotions, and cosmetics.

Function: Prevents microbial growth and extends shelf life.

 

Benzalkonium Chloride

Usage: Found in eye drops and disinfectants, exhibiting strong antibacterial properties.

Function: Keeps products clean and prevents bacterial infection.

 

Benzethonium Chloride

Usage: Used in topical disinfectants and skin ointments.

Function: Offers antimicrobial action to ensure the safety of the product.

 

3. Solvents & Emulsifiers

Solvents and emulsifiers help dissolve active ingredients and improve stability, ensuring effective drug absorption.

 

Dimethyl Sulfoxide (DMSO)

Usage: Used in injectables and topical formulations.

Function: Increases drug permeability, enhancing absorption.

 

Oleic Acid

Usage: Used as a solvent in drug formulations to improve the solubility of lipophilic drugs.

Function: Increases drug bioavailability, enhancing its effects.

 

Aluminum Monostearate

Usage: Used in emulsions, creams, and oral liquids.

Function: Ensures the stability of emulsions, preventing separation or crystallization.

 

4. Thickening Agents & Sustained Release Agents

These excipients are used to control the release rate of drugs, ensuring long-lasting therapeutic effects.

 

Povidone K90

Usage: Used in tablets, capsules, and oral liquids.

Function: Enhances viscosity and controls drug release rates.

 

Cellulose Acetate

Usage: Commonly used in sustained-release tablets and formulations.

Function: Extends the release time of the drug, ensuring prolonged therapeutic effects.

 

Hydroxypropyl Methylcellulose (HPMC)

Usage: Used in sustained-release drugs and oral tablets.

Function: Enhances stability and controls the release of the drug.

 

5. Oral Dosage Form Excipients

These excipients are critical for the stability and absorption of oral dosage forms, ensuring effective drug delivery.

 

Gelatin

Usage: Used in capsule production to stabilize the drug.

Function: Improves drug release and bioavailability.

 

Crosspovidone

Usage: Used in tablets and capsules as a disintegrant.

Function: Speeds up the disintegration of the drug in the stomach, enhancing absorption.

 

Stearic Acid

Usage: Used as a lubricant in tablet and capsule manufacturing.

Function: Reduces friction and improves the efficiency of drug production.

 

6. Auxiliary Function Excipients

These excipients are used to improve drug flow, prevent foaming, and aid in suspending active ingredients, ensuring effective drug performance and production efficiency.

 

Colloidal Silicon Dioxide

Usage: Used to improve drug flow and assist with powder processing.

Function: Increases drug flowability, preventing clumping.

 

Talc

Usage: Used as a lubricant to reduce friction during drug production.

Function: Ensures smooth flow of tablets and capsules, preventing clogging.

 

Titanium Dioxide

Usage: Used as a coloring agent and opacifier in drugs.

Function: Ensures the stability and aesthetic appearance of the product.

 

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

 

1. How do I choose the right excipient for my drug?

The selection of the right excipient depends on the dosage form, therapeutic effect and absorption requirements of the drug. For example, for oral tablets, it is crucial to choose the right disintegrant and binder; for injections, the choice of solvent and buffer affects the stability and absorption rate of the drug. We recommend working with GOLIATH's technical team to ensure that the best excipient is selected for your drug.

 

2. How do the excipients provided by GOLIATH ensure the stability of the drug?

The excipients provided by GOLIATH are rigorously tested for stability. Excipients such as thickeners, emulsifiers, and antimicrobial preservatives can help ensure that the drug is not affected by environmental changes throughout the shelf life, avoiding drug degradation or microbial contamination. Our excipient products meet the standards of major global pharmacopoeias (such as USP, EP), ensuring the safety of drugs during long-term storage or transportation.

 

3. What are the key factors in the selection of excipients in oral tablets?

In oral tablets, excipients such as disintegrants, binders, and lubricants are crucial. Disintegrants help drugs disintegrate and be absorbed quickly in the gastrointestinal tract, binders ensure the structural integrity of tablets, and lubricants prevent mold jams and increase fluidity during the production process. Depending on the solubility, absorbability and other characteristics of the drug, the selection and proportion of excipients will vary.

 

4. How to ensure the safety of excipients for patients?

All excipients provided by GOLIATH are strictly quality controlled and meet international GMP standards. Our excipient products do not contain harmful substances and have undergone various toxicology and biocompatibility tests to ensure that there will be no side effects on patients during drug preparation and application.

 

5. Do excipients affect the bioavailability of drugs?

Yes, the choice of excipients directly affects the bioavailability of drugs. For example, solvents and emulsifiers help drugs dissolve better and increase absorption rate; while sustained-release agents can prolong the release time of drugs and improve therapeutic effects. GOLIATH's excipients provide a variety of types to help optimize the bioavailability of drugs and ensure maximum efficacy.
